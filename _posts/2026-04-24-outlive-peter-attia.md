**Outlive: The Science and Art of Longevity**  
By Peter Attia, MD (2023) · Physician-engineer’s operating manual for **healthspan** · Core idea: **Medicine 3.0 prevents chronic disease decades before symptoms—especially through exercise, nutrition, sleep, and emotional health treated as serious interventions**

## Overview

Peter Attia built a public following by asking uncomfortable questions: Why do we wait until the heart attack to care about lipids? Why is “exercise” a vague recommendation while oncology doses drugs to the milligram? *Outlive* is his attempt to name the enemy and the toolkit.

**Medicine 1.0** — treat infection and trauma.  
**Medicine 2.0** — chronic disease management after diagnosis.  
**Medicine 3.0** — **risk math + prevention** for the diseases that actually kill most adults.

The book centers on the **Four Horsemen** (atherosclerosis, cancer, neurodegeneration, metabolic dysfunction) and five tactical domains: **exercise, nutrition, sleep, emotional health, exogenous molecules** (drugs/supplements when evidence warrants).

Attia’s motivational frame is the **Centenarian Decathlon**: train today for the physical tasks you want at 90—not merely survive to 90 depleted.

Three questions while reading: **Which Horseman is my highest quantified risk? Is my exercise program covering all four buckets? Am I borrowing from tomorrow’s health for today’s comfort?**

## Exercise: the four-part prescription

Attia’s exercise chapter is the book’s operational core:

| Bucket | Target | Why |
|--------|--------|-----|
| **Stability** | Balance, eccentric control, fall avoidance | “Margin of safety” in Centenarian Decathlon |
| **Strength** | Muscle mass and bone density | Metabolic organ; independence |
| **Zone 2** | Aerobic base, mitochondrial health | Fat oxidation, cardiac output |
| **VO2 max** | Peak aerobic power | Strong mortality association |

```text
Week template (conceptual — individualize)
3–4× Zone 2 sessions (talk test: nasal breathing possible)
1× VO2 / high-intensity intervals (when cleared)
3× strength (compound lifts + carries)
Daily stability snacks (see Built to Move)
```

[Starting Strength]({{ '/2026/06/06/starting-strength-rippetoe/' | relative_url }}) covers **strength novice phase**; Attia tells you why you still need Zone 2 after LP ends. [NROL]({{ '/2026/06/19/new-rules-of-lifting-schuler-cosgrove/' | relative_url }}) offers staged lifting variety; [Built to Move]({{ '/2026/06/08/built-to-move-starrett/' | relative_url }}) supplies daily stability practice.

## Centenarian Decathlon

Instead of abstract “fitness,” list **tasks at 90**:

- Hike 1.5 miles on uneven ground  
- Get up from floor with one point of contact  
- Carry two bags of groceries five blocks  
- Climb four flights of stairs  
- Play on floor with a child  

Train **backward** from those events: which muscle, energy system, and skill each demands?

## Nutrition (principles, not a fad diet)

Attia emphasizes:

- **Protein adequacy** especially with age (muscle preservation)  
- **Glycemic control** individualized—CGM stories illustrate, not mandate  
- **Energy balance** without reckless crash diets  
- **Sustainability** over performative restriction  

Pair quantitative curiosity with [有效复盘]({{ '/2026/05/24/effective-retrospective/' | relative_url }})—monthly review of labs, sleep, and training logs.

## Sleep and emotional health

Sleep is framed as **cardiovascular and cognitive medicine**, not laziness. Emotional health includes **stress, shame, anger, relationships**—Attia’s personal chapters model vulnerability without replacing therapy.

For skills: [DBT]({{ '/2026/05/19/dbt-skills-handouts-linehan/' | relative_url }}), [NVC]({{ '/2026/06/01/nonviolent-communication-rosenberg/' | relative_url }}), professional support when needed.

## Critiques worth keeping

- **Length and repetition** — core ideas could be a long essay.  
- **Privilege lens** — VO2 lab tests and concierge medicine are not universal.  
- **Evolving science** — verify pharmacology against current guidelines.  
- **Memoir sections** — powerful but emotionally heavy; pace yourself.

Still: for many readers, *Outlive* is the first time **exercise dose** felt as serious as **drug dose**.
