**The New Rules of Lifting**  
By Lou Schuler & Alwyn Cosgrove (1st ed., 2006; later editions and spin-offs include *NROL for Women*, *NROL for Life*, *Supercharged*) · A journalist–coach collaboration that made **sensible strength training** legible to people who had only ever done cardio and machines · Core idea: **lift weights with purpose, vary the stimulus, recover, and stop chasing the scale**

## Overview

Before *Starting Strength* became the default barbell answer, *The New Rules of Lifting* (NROL) filled a different gap: the gym member who knew they should lift but was drowning in **bodybuilding magazine chaos**—high reps forever, endless cardio, no progression logic, and “fat loss” confused with **weight loss**.

Schuler (writer) and Cosgrove (coach) translate Alwyn’s gym floor into rules a normal adult can follow:

> Fat loss is a **hormonal and behavioral** outcome; the scale lies. Muscle and strength are assets you build in **stages**, not random workouts.

NROL’s signature move is **periodized variety**: hypertrophy phases, strength phases, metabolic density work, and planned **fat-loss breaks**—always anchored in **basic movement patterns** (squat, hinge, push, pull, core) with barbells, dumbbells, and bodyweight.

Three questions while reading: **Which stage am I in and why? Am I getting stronger or just sweatier? Does my program respect recovery?**

## The six rules (spirit of the series)

Exact wording shifts by edition; the through-line:

| Rule | Meaning | Failure mode |
|------|---------|--------------|
| **Lift weights** | Resistance is non-negotiable for body comp | Pink dumbbells forever |
| **Don’t live on cardio** | Intervals/conditioning yes; hamster wheel no | Cardio compensating for diet |
| **Vary reps & loads** | Stimulus must change across phases | Same 12 reps for years |
| **Use basic patterns** | Squat/hinge/push/pull/core | Random “muscle confusion” |
| **Recover** | Muscles grow between sessions | Daily exhaustion as virtue |
| **Change the program** | Planned progression, not chaos | Program hopping weekly |

## Stage model (original NROL)

Typical arc across **weeks**, not days:

```text
Stage I   — Hypertrophy: learn patterns, moderate reps, build work capacity
Stage II  — Strength: lower reps, heavier loads
Stage III — Metabolic: density, shorter rest, conditioning emphasis
Stage IV  — Power / athletic: speed and explosiveness (as appropriate)
Fat-loss break — Nutrition + training tweak between macrocycles
```

Each stage has prescribed **sets, reps, rest, and exercise menus**. You are not guessing on Monday morning.

### Movement menu (conceptual)

| Pattern | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| Squat | Goblet squat, barbell squat, split squat |
| Hinge | Deadlift variations, Romanian deadlift |
| Push | Bench, overhead press, push-up |
| Pull | Row, chin-up/lat pulldown |
| Core | Planks, rollouts, anti-rotation work |

## Fat loss vs weight loss

NROL’s cultural contribution is insisting:

- **Scale down + performance up** can coexist (recomposition).  
- **Crash dieting + high volume** trades muscle for temporary weight.  
- Measure **tape, photos, strength, energy**—not only pounds.

This pairs cleanly with [Outlive]({{ '/2026/04/24/outlive-peter-attia.html' | relative_url }})’s metabolic lens without copying Attia’s medical depth.
